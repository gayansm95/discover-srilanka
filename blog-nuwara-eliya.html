Sri Lanka's Little England : Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya, often called “Little England,” is a charming hill-country town in Sri Lanka that attracts many foreign travelers with its cool climate, misty mountains, tea plantations, and colonial charm. Here are the top places to visit in Nuwara Eliya that are especially popular with international visitors.

Gregory Lake

Gregory Lake is one of the main attractions in Nuwara Eliya. Surrounded by green hills, it’s perfect for relaxing walks, boat rides, and enjoying the cool weather. Foreign travelers often enjoy picnics, cycling, and photography here, especially in the evening.

Tea Plantations & Tea Factories

Nuwara Eliya is at the heart of Sri Lanka’s tea country. Visiting a tea plantation or tea factory allows travelers to see how world-famous Ceylon tea is produced. Guided tours usually end with a fresh tea tasting and scenic views of endless green hills.

Hakgala Botanical Garden

Located a short drive from town, Hakgala Botanical Garden is one of the highest-altitude botanical gardens in Sri Lanka. It is home to roses, orchids, ferns, and exotic plants. The peaceful setting makes it ideal for nature lovers and leisurely walks.

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit for adventurous travelers. The park is famous for World’s End, a dramatic cliff with breathtaking views, and Baker’s Falls. The early-morning hike through misty grasslands is a memorable experience for foreign visitors.

Victoria Park

Located in the town center, Victoria Park is a beautifully maintained park ideal for relaxing. It’s popular among bird watchers and travelers who want a peaceful break after sightseeing.

Nuwara Eliya Town & Colonial Architecture

Walking around Nuwara Eliya town itself is a unique experience. Colonial-era buildings, the old post office, and traditional English-style houses give the town its distinctive charm.
