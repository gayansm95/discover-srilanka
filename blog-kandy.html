Kandy - The Cultural Capital

Sri Lanka's Last Royal Kingdom

Nestled among emerald hills in Sri Lanka's central highlands, Kandy is a city that breathes history, spirituality, and natural beauty. Once the last royal capital of the island, Kandy today stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a living cultural treasure where ancient rituals blend seamlessly with scenic charm.

This enchanting city, perched 500 meters above sea level, offers a perfect blend of cultural immersion and natural splendor. From the rhythmic beating of traditional drums echoing through ancient temples to the fragrant scent of incense wafting through misty air, Kandy promises an authentic Sri Lankan experience that engages all your senses.

Sri Dalada Maligawa

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic

At the heart of Kandy lies the sacred Sri Dalada Maligawa (Temple of the Tooth Relic), one of the most revered Buddhist sites in the world. This magnificent temple houses the sacred tooth relic of Lord Buddha, believed to have been brought to Sri Lanka in the 4th century AD, hidden in the hair of a princess.

The temple complex is a masterpiece of Kandyan architecture, featuring intricate wood carvings, painted ceilings, and golden decorations. The rhythmic sound of traditional drums, the scent of lotus flowers, and the warm glow of oil lamps during evening puja (worship ceremony) create a deeply moving spiritual atmosphere.

Peradeniya Royal Botanical Garden

Asia's Finest Botanical Paradise

Spread across 147 acres of lush land beside the Mahaweli River, the Peradeniya Royal Botanical Garden is one of Asia's finest botanical gardens. Originally established in the 14th century as a royal garden for Kandyan kings, it was later developed by the British into the magnificent living museum it is today.

Wander through avenues lined with towering royal palm trees, discover exotic orchids in the orchid house, marvel at the giant Javan fig tree with its sprawling canopy covering 1,600 square meters, and relax in the fragrant spice garden. The garden is home to over 4,000 species of plants, including many rare and endemic varieties.

Kandy Lake

The Heart-Shaped Lake of Royalty

Created in 1807 by the last king of Kandy, Sri Wickrama Rajasinghe, this picturesque artificial lake is the centerpiece of the city. Known locally as "Kiri Muhuda" (Sea of Milk), the lake was constructed to enhance the beauty of the Temple of the Tooth and provide a serene setting for royal contemplation.

A gentle 3.4-kilometer walk around the lake reveals Kandy's softer sideâ€”watch locals fishing in traditional boats, monks meditating on the shores, and families enjoying evening strolls. As the sun sets, reflections of hills and temples shimmer on the water, creating perfect moments for quiet reflection and photography. The lake is home to various bird species, including cormorants, egrets, and kingfishers, making it a birdwatcher's delight.

Ambuluwawa Tower

360-Degree Panoramic Paradise

Rising dramatically above the landscape at 1,087 meters (3,567 feet), Ambuluwawa Tower offers the most breathtaking 360-degree views of Kandy and beyond. This multi-religious spiritual center features a unique architectural blend of Buddhist, Hindu, Muslim, and Christian elements, symbolizing Sri Lanka's religious harmony.

The winding climb through spiraling staircases rewards visitors with panoramic vistas of mountains, forests, tea plantations, and distant villages. On clear days, you can see as far as the Knuckles Mountain Range and even the coast. The tower stands amidst a biodiversity complex with rare plant species, medicinal herbs, and lush greenery, making it both a spiritual and ecological destination.

Nelligala International Buddhist Center

Hilltop Sanctuary in the Clouds

Perched high on a hilltop at 2,500 feet above sea level, Nelligala International Buddhist Center offers spiritual serenity amidst clouds. The golden stupa, often surrounded by morning mist, provides a tranquil space for meditation and offers some of the most stunning sunrise views in Sri Lanka.

This modern Buddhist complex, established in 2005, features beautiful gardens, meditation halls, a museum, and panoramic viewpoints. The center promotes inter-religious harmony and offers visitors a peaceful retreat from city life. The climb to the summit is rewarded with breathtaking views of the Victoria Reservoir, Knuckles Mountain Range, and surrounding villages.

Esala Dalada Perahera

Asia's Grandest Cultural Festival

Kandy's cultural soul shines brightest during the Esala Dalada Perahera, one of Asia's grandest and most spectacular festivals. For ten nights in July or August (coinciding with the full moon), the streets of Kandy come alive with a dazzling celebration of faith, tradition, and artistic expression that dates back to the 4th century AD.

The procession features hundreds of exquisitely costumed dancers, traditional drummers, fire performers, whip crackers, and over 100 beautifully adorned elephants. The Maligawa Tusker carries a golden casket containing a replica of the sacred tooth relic, accompanied by temple officials, musicians, and flag bearers. Each night, the procession grows in grandeur, culminating in the final night's spectacular display attended by thousands of locals and tourists.

Festival Planning Tips

Accommodation

Book 6-12 months in advance as hotels fill up quickly during festival season.

Viewing Seats

Reserve premium seating along the procession route through authorized agents.

Photography

Bring a good camera with low-light capabilities. Tripods may be restricted.

Weather Preparedness

Carry a light rain jacket as evening showers are common in July/August.
